Data Management and Privacy in Health Research

An essential guide to responsible data handling in health research, covering storage, privacy, de-identification, and legal compliance.

Published by SHP Research Methods Team
15 minute
Updated March 12, 2025

Who is this for

Health researchers, clinical trial coordinators, data managers, and students working with sensitive health data who need to understand best practices in data security and privacy.

Key Takeaways

Understand legal and ethical obligations for handling health data
Learn how to securely store and manage participant data
Explore de-identification strategies and data sharing considerations
Recognize the role of data governance in research integrity

Protecting personal health information is not only a legal requirement but also a cornerstone of ethical research.Data Management and Privacy in Health Researchoffers clear, practical guidance on how to handle sensitive data throughout the research lifecycle. This resource explores key topics including data collection protocols, storage and access control, de-identification techniques, and compliance with privacy legislation such as the Privacy Act and relevant state or institutional frameworks. Designed with researchers in mind, this resource equips you with the confidence to manage data securely and ethically, ensuring research integrity and participant trust.

Transcript
Transcript

Dr. Melissa Nguyen: One of the most common mistakes in data management is underestimating how identifiable even 'anonymous' data can be. Thats why de-identification needs to go beyond names and addressesit involves removing or modifying any data that could reasonably re-identify someone.

A/Prof. David Chen: And remember, data security isnt just about locked folders. Its about access protocols, audit trails, and training your team. If youre collecting data on mobile devices or transferring between systems, you need end-to-end encryption and clear SOPs.

About the Presenters

Dr. Melissa Nguyen Data Governance Lead, Sydney Local Health District; specialises in privacy frameworks and secure data infrastructure.

A/Prof. David Chen Implementation Scientist and Clinical Trialist; experienced in designing ethical research protocols and data sharing models in multisite studies.
